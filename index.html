Hello! My name is Rui-Jie (pronounced "Roo-Jee"), and I'm an S.M. student in the Algorithmic Alignment Group at MIT CSAIL. My current research interests are in the human-centered and legal aspects of computation. This includes the analysis and design of regulation for emerging technologies, as well as a policy-informed, human-centered development of technical systems.

In 2021, I received a joint B.A. in computer science and mathematics from Scripps College as an off-campus student at Harvey Mudd College. I have also developed open-source differential privacy libraries as part of Google's anonymization team and researched facial processing technology case law as part of SonyAI's AI ethics team.

I'm excited to be an incoming PhD student in computer science at Brown University!
